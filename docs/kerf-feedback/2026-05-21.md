## [MAJOR] `kerf next` scoring collapses to two buckets; priority is invisible

**Command:** `kerf next --format=json --only=bead`

**Observed:** Of the 66 beads in today's feed, 50+ carry exactly `score=3.4857142857142858` (= 24.4 / 7) and the rest carry `score=0`. No intermediate values. Beads of very different priority and urgency tie:

- `hk-xegej` (P0, blocks the rest of the friction batch via the AssertEventCausality contract) → score 3.486
- `hk-bra0j` (P3, a follow-up hardening on `harmonik subscribe`) → score 3.486
- `hk-kumjl` (P3, a documentation note) → score 3.486

`br` priority (P0/P1/P2/P3) does not appear to feed into the score at all, even though it's the project's primary urgency signal. Per `kerf next --help`, the rubric is "dependency fan-out, momentum, rework, and creation order" — the absence of `br` priority looks intentional but produces a feed where the orchestrator can't distinguish "do this first" from "do this eventually."

**Impact:** Today's directive "kerf is the priority source of truth" cannot hold while the top-50 beads are all tied. The orchestrator falls back to reading `br show` on each candidate to recover the priority gradient — re-doing the work `kerf next` was supposed to do.

**Suggest:** Either fold `br` priority into the score (e.g. multiply by a priority weight), or document the deliberate choice to ignore it so users know to combine `kerf next` with a priority filter (`br ready --priority=0,1`).

**Repro:**
```
kerf next --format=json --only=bead \
  | jq '.items[] | select(.kind=="bead") | .score' \
  | sort | uniq -c
```

## [NIT] `kerf pin` reports success but the bead does not appear in `kerf next` until status flips to `open`

**Command:** `kerf pin phase-2-completion hk-cd8yu` then `kerf next`

**Observed:** Pin succeeds (`Pinned hk-cd8yu to phase-2-completion.`). Bead does not appear in `kerf next`. Root cause turned out to be that hk-cd8yu was `in_progress` at the time and `kerf next` correctly filters to ready beads only.

**Expected (NIT):** Either `kerf pin` could warn when pinning a non-ready bead ("Pinned, but hk-cd8yu is in_progress and won't appear in `kerf next` until it returns to open"), or `kerf next` could surface a one-line summary count of "N attached beads currently in_progress / closed" so the absence is explained without the user grepping `br show`.

This is a NIT because the underlying behavior is correct; the surprise is purely in the silence.
